## Background
I was taught that the balance of the Fey Lords was sacred — that their enduring order was the thin line keeping the world from collapse. Stability was the highest virtue, and those who threatened it, whether mortal or divine, were to be watched, managed, or silenced.
  
But the more secrets I uncovered, the more I realized that divine order is no cleaner than mortal ambition. The same intrigues, rivalries, bargains, and quiet cruelties that shape syndicates and courts below are mirrored in the powers above. I was not taught a lie exactly — I was taught a truth so carefully edited that it became a weapon.
