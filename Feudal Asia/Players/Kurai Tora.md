# Backstory
## Part 1: A Daughter of the Volcanic Wilds
High in the shadow of the twin volcanoes, Kazan-Tetsu ("Iron Mountain") and Kazan-Kiri ("Mist Mountain"), the nomadic Ikh Zaya tribe thrived for generations. These stewards of the wilds revered the volcanoes as sacred thresholds between the mortal and spiritual realms. The mountains embodied the delicate balance of existence: Tetsu’s resilience and strength contrasted with Kiri’s shifting, transient nature.

Among the tribe’s hunters and guardians was Kurai Tora, whose name means "Dark Tiger." From a young age, she showed an affinity for the quiet patience of a predator, a talent for reading the land, and an uncanny precision with the bow. She became a Shadow Warden, charged with patrolling the tribe’s sacred territories, tracking threats, and restoring balance when spiritual or natural forces were disturbed.

The volcanoes loomed large in Kurai’s life. From Tetsu, she drew strength and determination, finding solace in its jagged, unyielding peaks. From Kiri, she learned the value of subtlety and change, her steps as silent and adaptable as the shifting mists. These twin forces shaped her into a warrior both steadfast and unpredictable, a silent wind that could harden into a gale.

But her life was shattered when Yokai—a malevolent force the tribe had always guarded against—attacked. The Ikh Zaya were slaughtered, their sacred sites defiled. Kurai, the lone survivor, fled into the wilds, wounded and desperate. In her darkest hour, she stumbled upon an ancient shrine beneath Kazan-Kiri, where her destiny would change forever.
## Part 2: The Kami and the Call to Kinkou
The shrine Kurai found was home to [[Kuroyo]], the Whispering Gale, a dragon Kami of twilight and winds. This ethereal being, coiling its translucent form around the ruins, recognized Kurai’s pain and potential. In a moment of desperation and communion, Kuroyo offered her a pact: her survival in exchange for her loyalty to balance. Kurai accepted, and the Kami’s essence became bound to a longbow she found at the shrine.

This bow, now called Aeryn, became a vessel for Kuroyo’s power, growing stronger with every trial Kurai faced. Its arrows carried the breath of the dragon—silent yet devastating, precise as the wind. Kuroyo’s whispers guided Kurai’s path, urging her toward the city of Kinkou, where the balance between mortals and Kami had grown dangerously unstable.

Kurai did not want to go. The city, visible from the slopes of the volcanoes, had always seemed distant and foreign, a place of noise and rigidity. Yet Kuroyo’s presence left no doubt: her purpose now lay among the narrow streets and towering temples of Kinkou.
## Part 3: The Shadow Wind in Kinkou
Kurai’s first days in the city were jarring. The crowded streets, incessant noise, and rigid hierarchies of Kinkou were unlike the open skies and fluid traditions of her tribe. She felt alien and unwelcome, more ghost than guest. Her wild grace drew wary glances, and her connection to the Kami—a bond revered among her people—was treated with suspicion by many of the city’s residents.

But Kurai’s skills quickly proved her worth. When a Yokai began preying on the outskirts of Kinkou, the Temple District Clan, the spiritual stewards of the city, called upon her as a last resort. In the dead of night, Kurai tracked the creature through alleys and rooftops, setting an ambush from an impossible perch. With a single arrow loosed from Aeryn, she struck the Yokai down, leaving its body as proof of her skill.

That act earned her a place within the Temple District Clan as a Shadow Protector, a trusted enforcer of balance between mortals and Kami. Over time, her reputation grew: tales of her impossible shots, her ability to move unseen through the city, and her quiet yet unwavering devotion to the Kami spread like whispers on the wind.

Though respected, Kurai remained an outsider. The clan valued her results but often struggled with her independence and unorthodox methods. To Kurai, the city was a new kind of wilderness, and she navigated it on her own terms, using its structures and shadows as tools in her hunt for imbalance.
# Part 4: Kurai Tora Among New Allies
Kurai Tora carries herself like the mist of Kazan-Kiri—calm, silent, and elusive. She is often reserved, observing before speaking, and prefers action to conversation. Yet beneath her stoic demeanor lies the heart of a tribal guardian, one who understands the strength of a group bound by trust and purpose.

In a new party, Kurai operates autonomously but keeps a watchful eye on her allies, ready to defend or guide them when needed. She has little patience for recklessness or dishonesty but admires those who prove their mettle and honor. While she rarely shares her past, her actions speak of a deep, unspoken grief and a fierce loyalty to those she chooses to protect.

Kurai Tora may be a shadow in the wind, but she is also a constant presence—quiet, steady, and deadly. Her arrows never miss their mark, and her instincts rarely lead her astray. To stand beside her is to know that, though unseen, a protector always watches.
