# Backstory
Born into humble beginnings, Ji Jon Wo was once a young and restless halfling with dreams of fortune and influence. Over time, his travels as a merchant and diplomat led him to become the respected leader of the Trade District Clan, one of the most influential families in the metropolis. Though his rise to power was driven by cunning and strategy, Ji now values balance and peace above wealth, striving to guide his clan and city toward harmony.
# Appearance
A halfling in his early fifties, Ji carries the wisdom of his years with an unassuming, dignified air. His robes, while modest in cut, shimmer with the rich colors of his clan—deep golds and reds. The intricate symbols of his station are embroidered with fine thread, and his gloves, worn through countless negotiations and battles, mark him as someone both respected and capable.
# Mannerisms
Ji speaks softly and deliberately, choosing his words with care. He often seems to have a wry smile or a knowing look, as if he’s privy to the punchline of a joke no one else understands. While he’s deeply respectful of tradition and ceremony, he’ll occasionally surprise others with a sharp, clever remark or a light tease, especially when it’s least expected. He often remains silent in tense situations, allowing others to speak first—before he makes his move.
# Aorihan and the Gloves of Forceful Accord
Ji’s connection to [[Aorihan]], a fox spirit and Kami of the Land, is one of deep reverence and mutual respect. Though Aorihan sometimes fancies themselves an aspect of the Kami of Balance and Prosperity, Ji often finds humor in their grandiose statements. The Gloves of Forceful Accord—worn by Ji for years—are more than just a symbol of his leadership. The gloves are a tangible link to his diplomatic prowess and martial skill, enhancing his physical strength and constitution, and enabling him to strike with both precision and force when needed.
# Relation with Fukumi
Ji and Fukumi are quite close, in a foster-parent - child relationship (“Uncle Ji” and “Fuki”). Ji worked as first mate on one of the ships of Fukumi’s father. When returning to port, Ji and Renshu would meet Fukumi’s father to exchange news and instructions for the next voyage. Sometimes, he would bring Fukumi to these meetings, and Ji and Fukumi would spar a little. Eventually, at age 14, Fukumi escaped her forced marriage with Ji’s assistance, mediated through her governess, a common acquaintance. With knowledge gleaned from Kurai, Ji brought her to the Baatar clan for safekeeping, and visited her from time to time to see if she was doing well. After her return to Kinkou, they would occasionally meet up to catch up on life events.
