# Background
Fukumi is rumored to have been born in Kinkou to noble origins. Her refined manners and extensive education in history, religion, and etiquette lend credence to these whispers, though the truth remains elusive. It is said she escaped an arranged marriage as a child, severing all ties to her birth family, but no one can confirm this. Stories of her past abound, each more fantastical than the last, ensuring her name is a favorite among the sages’ speculative tales.

Wherever she’s from originally, she is known to be adopted into the Baatar tribe as a teenager. The nomadic Baatar tribe roams mineral-rich pastures on the misty slopes of the volcano Kazan-Kiri, producing meats that are celebrated across Kinkou. The tribe is not only known for their goods but for their role as protectors of the northern frontier, defending the region from bandits and yokai alike. Fukumi was adopted into the tribe’s leading family, where her impulsive nature was tempered by her step-father’s warmth and wisdom. His teachings of leadership and restraint echo in her every action, while her step-mother, a legendary yokai hunter, shaped her into a fearsome battle commander.

In a tragic turn of events, her step-mother met her end while trapping a mad yokai in a cave. In vengeful desperation, the yokai collapsed the cave, sealing both their fates. The loss of her step-mother led Fukumi to seek solace at a solemn shrine near Kazan-Kiri’s peak, where she encountered [[Yūki no Kaze no Kami]], a serpentine dragon-like Kami made of clouds. Yūki’s playful spirit helped her overcome her grief, and together they forged a bond of joy and freedom. This bond deepened into a pact: to confront the yokai menaces and champion the freedom that both Fukumi and Yūki cherished. Yūki took the form of a Naginata, becoming Fukumi’s constant companion in combat and life.

Called back to Kinkou by the Emperor to aid in the city’s defense against the rising yokai threat, Fukumi reluctantly left her tribe behind. Her strategic brilliance and battlefield leadership quickly earned her respect in the city, though she often deflects questions about her success with cryptic phrases like, “Opportunities multiply as they are seized,” and “Water shapes its course according to the ground.” Her unconventional tactics and graceful combat style have made her a trusted advisor and the spearhead of Kinkou’s elite counter-yokai unit. Though she yearns for the mountains and her tribe, Fukumi finds solace in the city’s gardens and libraries, her sense of duty driving her to protect Kinkou’s people from the growing darkness.
# Mannerisms
Fukumi is typically discreet and polite, often choosing to observe before speaking. When she does speak, her words are measured and leave a lasting impression. She demonstrates a mastery of social etiquette, reflecting her noble upbringing, though moments of juvenile impatience occasionally break through. In such instances, her composed facade falters, and she speaks with disarming frankness, often catching others off guard with her blunt honesty.

Her movements are deliberate and strikingly graceful, reminiscent of a dancer’s poise. Every step and gesture is purposeful, with no excess or flourish. Whether in combat or conversation, Fukumi’s physical presence conveys a balance of elegance and restraint, a reflection of both her tactical mind and disciplined training.

# Bond met [[Yūki no Kaze no Kami|Yūki]] (DM note)
Je band met Yuki no Kaze no Kami kan diep zijn, maar wat zij met je uitwisselt is wellicht in byzondere momenten een visioen oid, en meestal eerder een gevoel van goed- of afkeuring, blijdschap of verdriet. Het laatste dat je hebt gemerkt in de tijd dat je in de stad bent, is dat Yuki no Kaze no Kami iets anders op je lijkt te reageren, dan voorheen buiten de stad, maar je hebt nog niet echt een gevoel waardoor dat komt, en wijt het maar aan de veranderingen die de stad met zich meebrengt. Net zoals jij je nog moeilijk kan voegen hier, is dat voor je kami wellicht ook zo.
