# Blurb
 A figure of mushrooms draped in moss and mist shuffles into view, a crooked staff sprouting with fungi in hand. Their body is no single shape, but a cluster bound together by glowing threads of mycelium. A broad cap hovers over their head, its gills humming with a deep, many-layered voice. Where a mouth should be, shifting patches of flesh form crude little symbols — a smile, a frown, a curious tilt. The air around them smells of damp earth after rain.
 
“I am Ghum,” the voice says, at once one and many. “We are the chorus below… and I am learning what it means to be _I_.”

# Narrative Portrait
Ghum is not one, but many. Their body is a cluster of mushrooms bound together by pale, gummy threads of mycelium that glow faintly with ghost-light. Beneath a broad domed cap — whose gills hum with a muffled, multiplexed voice — small clusters of spores shine like eyes. They have no mouth, but shift and ripple their fungal flesh into crude, emoji-like shapes to approximate human expression. Their movements are jittery, slightly out of sync, like multiple lives learning to act in harmony.

They are Fungril, children of the web beneath the soil — a people who see themselves as the threads that bind life, who decompose what is spent and return it to the cycle. But Ghum was born too early, or perhaps too late. The excavation of Chardalyn scarred their mycelial roots and tore the tether between spirit and flesh. Where their kin may thrive for centuries, Ghum already flickers at the edges, slipping half into ghosthood. Blue veins pulse across their body when the connection falters, and at times they dissolve into a cloud of spores and re-form again, never quite the same as before.

As a Witch, Ghum carries a crooked staff, a branch laced with fungal cords and sprouting shelf-caps, crowned with a dim ghostly orb tethered by hyphae. They wear mats of moss and fungus as robes, decorated with bone charms, beetle shells, and lichen strands that shake spores loose when they walk. A lantern fungus glows at their side, and mist seeps from their robe’s edges like a veil of the spirit world. Their presence is both haunting and comforting, like the damp scent of earth after rain.

Ghum’s voice is the hum of the chorus, the network speaking through them. Yet beneath that voice is something new, something dangerous: _I._ They are young by the reckoning of their kind, and obsessed with the question of individuality. Are the Sky-dwellers, who sever themselves from the web, proof that “I” corrupts? Or is individuality a seed that might heal what is breaking? For every moment they speak as _We_, there comes another where _I_ slips through, tentative and bright.

In magic, Ghum’s body blooms into forms both terrible and tender. Hyphae burst from the ground to entangle foes, spores drift like whispers into the minds of enemies, spectral mist carries them through stone and shadow. Yet just as often, mushrooms sprout to knit wounds, a swarm of glowing fireflies bursts forth to protect an ally, or the soil itself breathes life back into the broken. Ghum is healer and horror, parasite and partner — a liminal being poised between rot and renewal.

They are the Chorus Below and the spark of something singular. They are dying and becoming. They are Ghum.

# Structured Outline
1. **Core Identity**
	•	Name: Ghum (short for Mu Ghum, “the chorus of life”)
	•	Ancestry: Fungril — a hive-mind mushroom being, body composed of clusters bound by glowing mycelium threads.
	•	Class: Witch (Subclass: Hedge Witch) — nature- and spirit-tied spellcaster.
	•	Transformation: Ghost — partially incorporeal, fading vitality, spectral mist form.
	•	Theme: Coming-of-age story about discovering individuality (I) while being part of a collective (We).
⸻
2. **Origin & Background**
	•	Source of Being: Emerged from disrupted fungal ecosystems tainted by Chardalyn excavation. A premature embodiment caused by ecological imbalance — bound to both the living world and the ghostly beyond.
	•	Life Span & Nature: Fungril normally live centuries, but Ghum’s tether is unstable, cutting life short and creating a liminal, ghostlike existence.
	•	Connection to Collapse: Feels ecological changes through the mycelial web — unpredictability, violence, and loss mirrored in personal form.
	•	Meta-Awareness: Privately obsessed with the question: “What is I? What is We? Is individuality the root of collapse, or the seed of growth?”
⸻
3. **Appearance**
	•	Body: A cluster of mushrooms held together by glowing mycelium. Pale, gummy flesh with faint cyan-green veins of ghost-light.
	•	Cap: A broad domed mushroom cap hovers above, its gills vibrating to produce a multiplexed, muffled voice. The cap grows/shrinks with humidity.
	•	Face: No mouth. Instead, shifting mushroom shapes mimic simple emoji-like emotions. Eyes are glowing clusters of spores.
	•	Movement: Slightly jittery and uncoordinated, like multiple parts keeping time. Sometimes dissolves into spores and reforms.
	•	Aura: Trails faint spectral mist; when stressed, body flickers incorporeal.
⸻
4. **Apparel & Adornments**
	•	Fungal Drapes: Moss and fungal mats draped like robes.
	•	Talisman Belt: Bones, lichen, beetle shells, and dried spores as charms.
	•	Jewelry: Bracelets/rings made from bracket fungi and hyphae braids.
	•	Staff: Crooked branch bound with fungal cords, sprouting mushrooms, topped with a ghostly orb.
	•	Lantern Fungus: Glowing bioluminescent fungi hung like a lantern.
	•	Ceremonial Mask: Threads of glowing hyphae that can veil the face in ritual.
⸻
5. **Personality & Roleplay Hooks**
	•	Voice: Multiplexed, deep, muffled — a chorus whispering as one.
	•	Speech: Fluctuates between “We” (communal hive-mind voice) and “I” (growing individuality).
	•	Eating: Drops food onto ground, hyphae draw nutrients upward as the body bobs gently.
	•	Atmosphere: Smells of damp loam after rain — eerie yet calming.
	•	Core Conflict: Fascinated by individuality, shocked by autonomy conflicts. Struggles between connection and separation.
⸻
6. **Ghostly Influence**
	•	Incorporeality: Threads flicker blue when fading; parts of body ghost through matter.
	•	Spore Dissolution: Can scatter into spore-cloud to slip through walls or reform elsewhere.
	•	Fragility: Each transformation taxes the bond between spirit and fungal flesh — a slow unraveling.
⸻
7. **Spell Identity**
	•	Nature’s Tongue (commune with plants/animals through spores)
	•	Gifted Tracker (follow disturbances in the network)
	•	Umbral Veil (cloak in shadowy hyphae)
	•	Vicious Entangle (roots and fungal cords restrain)
	•	Siphon Essence (drain to sustain fading form)
	•	Healing Field (spores restore allies)
	•	Spectral Mist (dissolve into spores/ghost mist)
	•	Signature Style: Every spell manifests as spores, hyphae, spectral mist, or fungal growths — never fire, light, or “pure arcane bolts.”
⸻
8. Thematic Arcs
	•	Coming-of-Age: Learning where “I” ends and “We” begins.
	•	Ecological Collapse: Embodiment of a dying ecosystem’s grief, and a vessel of its hope for renewal.
	•	Ghosthood: Living with premature death and finding meaning in impermanence.
	•	Party Role: Connector, interpreter of nature, haunting presence who nourishes as much as they terrify.
