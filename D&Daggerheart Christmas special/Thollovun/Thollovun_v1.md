Thollovun is a major underground cave and the birthplace of [[Ghum]]. This name stems from [[Fungal Tongue]]:
- Thol: deep, beneath, hidden
- Lo: root, grounding, anchor
- Vun: fruiting, emergence, showing self

Outsiders, if familiar with the place, are more likely to know it by the name **Gardener’s Caves**. 
# Layout
Thollovun is situated in a cave deep underground. There are two main entries, joined by the river that has carved out both. One entry leads up top, towards the overworld, the other leads deeper into the underdark. Where the river enters the cave, it drops into a still lake, which mirrors the bioluminescent fungi in the ceiling. The river splits the cave into two sides. On one side, we find the main town. The walkable path alongside the river leads there. On the other side, we find the inner sanctum, only reachable by crossing a tough, spongelike structure called Ghumthum. Ghumthum's top is of chitin and strong enough to support those that want to cross it. Crossing it betrays the reason for the name: A feint, dull and slow pulse reverberates through body and ear when on top of it, channeled through pulsating, vine-like structures that can be found all across town.

Outside the well-protected main access tunnels, there's myriad smaller ones forming a maze only known to the Fungril of Thollovun.
## **🌊 Muneluu (The River)**
From the upper tunnels, the river plunges into the cavern as a waterfall, spilling into a still lake. The mirrored ceiling glows with bioluminescent fungi, making the whole cave shimmer like a second sky. A winding path descends slowly alongside the cavern wall, giving any traveler a panoramic view: the sprawling town on one side, the pulsing sanctum on the other.

The river splits Thollovun into two parts; on one site the village called Thollowem, and on the other the sacred site called Lothil, joined by an organic bridge called Ghumthum. The river disappears into a dark exit, leading to the underdark.
## **🏘️ Thollowem (The Body)**
Built along the riverbank and climbing into the cavern like a three-dimensional coral reef or termite mound, the town is a lattice of fungal chambers, bridges, and towers. Paths weave not only across the floor but up to the ceiling, forming a full vertical maze of living structures.

**Functions of Thollowem**
- **Gardeners & Herdsfolk**: Maintain fungal gardens, mulch pits, and herds of half-fungal beasts in pens around the outskirts.
- **Gatherers**: Bring organic matter from outer tunnels to feed the gardens.
- **Alchemists & Markets**: Work closer to the river path, distilling tinctures and trading in the spore plazas. These plazas double as spaces of gossip, ritualized storytelling, and exchange with outsiders.
- **Lorekeepers & Educators**: Keep memory tablets and schools here, teaching young sporelings the lessons of the Bloom.
- **Witches**: Though their true lairs are further in the maze-like side tunnels, some witches keep a small dwelling in town for trade and uneasy consultation.

**Tone**: Alien (somewhere in between cute and horror), but peaceful, contemplative, yet subtly guarded. Outsiders, though infrequent, are welcome to trade and converse, but always subtly steered away from Ghumthum.
## **💓 Ghumthum (The Heart)**
Ghumthum is the great, sponge-like structure spanning the river and linking the two halves of the cave.
- Its chitinous surface is firm enough to walk upon, but beneath, a porous structure embedded in the water supports it. Water and nutrients are pumped in slow pulses through vine-like conduits.
- Anyone who steps onto Ghumthum feels it: the dull, resonant _thrum_ that carries through their bones, as if walking on the heartbeat of the city.
- The vines spread throughout the town, delivering sustenance and reinforcing the sense that all parts of Thollovun are one living organism.

**Function**: A circulatory system — distributing water, nutrients, and life. Also regulates the lake's water level.
### **🌌 Lothil (The Mind)**
Beyond Ghumthum rises Lothil, the inner sanctum: a massive Chardalyn crystal whose veins run through the stone walls, feeding and glowing with fungal growths wrapped around it. Organic spires and lattices have fused with the crystal, forming sacred halls that feel more grown than built.

**Functions of Lothil**
- **Nexus Fungus**: The organism that links Fungril minds into a collective mesh, and the seat of their most sacred rites.
- **Council of Hyphae**: Decision-making takes place here, guided by both the voices of the people and the resonance of the Nexus.
- **Healers & Purifiers**: Work in proximity to the crystal, treating both Fungril bodies and the integrity of the network itself.
- **Midwives**: A specialized branch of Gardeners who cultivate lichen directly upon Chardalyn. From this, new Fungril are grown, carefully bred into subspecies according to the community’s needs.
- **Ritualists & Spiritual Guides**: Maintain ceremonies of remembrance, renewal, and communion with the Nexus.

**Tone**: Alien and sacred. Lothil is not merely architecture but a living cathedral of crystal and fungus, a mind made visible. Outsiders may only enter under the strictest necessity, and crossing Ghumthum without permission is unthinkable.

### **🕸️ The Maze of Side Tunnels**
Outside the main access tunnels, countless smaller passages twist away into the dark. These serve as hunting grounds, hidden trade paths, and secret lairs for witches. The maze is deliberately misleading to outsiders — only the Fungril know its ways.

# Ghum's home
[[Ghum]] lived in a smaller cave that can be reached through some smaller tunnels. It is located in between Lothil and [[The Glistening Quarry]]. It is lit by bioluminescent lichen and the soft purple glow of chardalyn veins that trace all along the surface. Having lived there for a while, it was filled with various defenses. Airborne spores that would make an intruder lose interest, or start experience horrific hallucinations when exposed for a long time. Carnivorous giant shrooms lay in hiding, camouflaged against walls and ceilings. Most of these cave defenses, as well as Ghum's own small dwelling in the center of the small cave, now lay under rubble after a excavation-induced collapse.
