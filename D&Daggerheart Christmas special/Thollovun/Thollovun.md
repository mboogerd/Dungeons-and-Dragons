Thollovun is a major underground cave and the birthplace of [[Ghum]]. Its name stems from [[Fungal Tongue]]:  
- **Thol**: deep, beneath, hidden  
- **Lo**: root, grounding, anchor  
- **Vun**: fruiting, emergence, showing self  

Outsiders, if familiar with the place at all, are more likely to know it by the name **Gardener’s Caves**. The general setting is one of alien and cute-horror. The Fungril have a different relation to the cycle of life than most humanoids, and this shows at every level: culture, politics, art, and customs.

---
## 🌊 **Muneluu (The River)**  
From the upper tunnels, the river plunges into the cavern as a waterfall, spilling into a still lake. The mirrored ceiling glows with bioluminescent fungi, making the whole cave shimmer like a second sky. A winding path descends along the cavern wall, giving travelers a panoramic view: the sprawling town of **Thollowem** on one side, the pulsing sanctum of **Lothil** on the other. The river disappears into a dark exit, leading deeper into the Underdark. Both exits are guarded against threats of any scale, though peaceful travellers are welcome.

---
## 🏘️ **Thollowem (The Body)**  
The town of Thollowem sprawls along the riverbank and climbs upward in a maze of chambers, bridges, and towers — part coral reef, part termite nest, but scaled for humanoid life. Paths run across the floor and up to the ceiling, creating a vertical labyrinth of fungal growths and living structures. 
### Life in Thollowem  
- **Gardeners & Herdsfolk** tend mulch pits, fungal gardens, and pens of beasts half-fused with fungi.  
- **Gatherers** bring carrion and detritus from the outer maze, feeding the gardens that in turn feed the people.  
- **Alchemists & Markets** cluster near the riverside, where tinctures, powders, and spore-brews are traded in glowing spore plazas.  
- **Lorekeepers & Educators** keep memory-tablets — fungal growths inscribed with ancestral spores — and teach sporelings their roles.  
- **Witches** are rarely seen here, but some maintain small huts at the edge of the maze, trading grim ingredients for alchemists’ coin.  

The tone of Thollowem is serene and courteous, almost zen-like, though alien to outsiders. Fungril speak slowly, their gestures weaving as though in rhythm with spores drifting in the air. Outsiders are tolerated and often welcomed here, but not easily trusted beyond what is polite.  

---
## 💓 **Ghumthum (The Heart)**  
Spanning the river, Ghumthum is a vast sponge-like structure with a chitinous surface firm enough to cross. Beneath its skin, water and nutrients are pumped through pulsating vines that spread across the city.  

Crossing Ghumthum betrays the reason for its name: a dull, resonant *thrum* that carries through bone and ear, like the heartbeat of the cavern itself. Its pulse reminds every Fungril that Thollovun is a living organism, not just a settlement.  

**Function**: A circulatory system — distributing water, nutrients, and life. Also regulates the lake's water level.

---
## 🌌 **Lothil (The Mind, The Inner Sanctum)**  
Beyond Ghumthum rises the sanctum of Lothil, dominated by a massive Chardalyn crystal whose veins run through the cavern walls. The crystal glows faintly with inner light, wrapped in lattices of fungus that form organic halls, spires, and chambers. This is the heart of Fungril identity, a place outsiders rarely get to approach.  

### Functions of the Sanctum  
- **Nexus Fungus**: Links Fungril minds into a collective mesh and stores ancestral resonance.  
- **Council of Hyphae**: Gather here to make decisions, weaving together the voices of the people with the hum of the Nexus.  
- **Healers & Purifiers**: Work in proximity to the crystal, tending both physical illness and corruption in the network.  
- **Midwives**: A specialized branch of Gardeners who cultivate sacred lichen directly upon Chardalyn. From these lichens, new Fungril are born — selectively bred to meet the needs of the community.  
- **Ritualists**: Lead ceremonies of remembrance, rebirth, and communion with the Nexus.  

Lothil is a cathedral of crystal and fungi, alien yet ordered. The walls hum faintly, voices of the mesh resonating like distant song.  

---
## 🕸️ The Outer Maze  
Beyond the main access tunnels lies a labyrinth of smaller passages. These are known only to the Fungril, serving as hunting grounds, hidden paths, and witches’ lairs. Outsiders who stray here find themselves disoriented, deterred by spore traps, fungal beasts, and defensive horrors woven into the stone.  

---
## ⚖️ The Divide  
- **Thollowem (Body)**: Produces food, trade goods, and learning.  
- **Ghumthum (Heart)**: Pumps water and nutrients, binding the settlement together.  
- **Lothil (Mind)**: Creates new Fungril, holds memory, guides decision and spirit.  

The town is open to outsiders — serene, polite, even welcoming — but the sanctum is not. Anyone attempting to cross Ghumthum without necessity will first be politely turned, then warned, then violently opposed.  

# Ghum's home
[[Ghum]] lived in a smaller cave that can be reached through some of the mazelike tunnels. It is located in between Lothil and [[The Glistening Quarry]]. It is lit by bioluminescent lichen and the soft purple glow of chardalyn veins that trace all along the surface. Having lived there for a while, it was filled with various defenses. Airborne spores that would make an intruder lose interest, or start experience horrific hallucinations when exposed for a long time. Carnivorous giant shrooms lay in hiding, camouflaged against walls and ceilings. Most of these cave defenses, as well as Ghum's own small dwelling in the center of the small cave, now lay under rubble after a excavation-induced collapse.
