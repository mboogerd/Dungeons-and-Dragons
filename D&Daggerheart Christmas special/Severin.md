# Blurb
Born among the **Orderborne**, a community of Netherese scholars who plumbed the depths of arcane theory, he was once a precocious student with ambitions larger than his station. Convinced he could achieve eternal glory where his mentors settled for gradual study, he seized their advanced instruments to attempt a dangerous shortcut — forging a pact with something that promised him brilliance and permanence. The result was not eternal youth, but a grotesque reanimation: a patchwork body that rots and reforms even as he clings to his aristocratic airs.

Now exiled, he styles himself as a **whistleblower**, claiming his corruption proves that Netheril’s magic is itself tainted, that Chardalyn and the Weave have been pushed too far. In every famine, tremor, and wildfire he sees vindication. He spreads these warnings with haughty conviction, presenting his changing body as _living proof_ of the world’s rot. Whether he is a genius unveiling truths the Orderborne dared not face, or merely a self-obsessed fool who blundered into undeath, is unclear — even to himself.

What is clear is his desire: **recognition**. To be remembered, to be proven right, and to rise above those who mocked him. And if the sky cities will not welcome him back in triumph, then he will take satisfaction in watching them fall to his level.
