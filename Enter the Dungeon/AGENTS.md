# AGENTS.md

## Scope
This file applies to `Enter the Dungeon/` and overrides the vault-level `AGENTS.md` for notes inside this campaign folder.

## Campaign Overview
`Enter the Dungeon` is a long-running campaign knowledge base organized around session history, world lore, faction notes, quests, people, locations, items, and symbols.

The campaign currently centers on CSI and long-running threads involving:
- `[[Neonetheril]]`
- `[[Sluitsteen]]`
- `[[Chardalyn]]`
- `[[Zoros]]`, `[[Talos]]`, and related storm or destructive power
- the current `[[Cormyr]]` / `[[Suzail]]` espionage arc

## How This Campaign Folder Is Maintained
This folder is maintained in layers.

Typical flow:
1. Session notes capture live play in chronological detail.
2. Quest notes track open objectives, leads, and practical next steps.
3. Person, location, item, and organization notes absorb confirmed facts over time.
4. Season summaries distill the arc into a clearer retrospective overview.

Important consequences:
- Session notes usually contain the most exact table-level truth.
- Summary notes are often more polished and may be written later.
- Entity notes are not always exhaustive; many are compact canon anchors rather than full biographies.
- It is normal for a sparse factual note and a richer summary elsewhere to coexist.

Do not force every note to become a full lore article.

## Source Of Truth
Use these in order when campaign continuity matters:
1. Relevant session notes in `Sessions/Season X/`
2. Season summary notes
3. Quest, person, organization, item, and location notes

If a summary conflicts with a session note, treat the session note as the stronger source and flag the mismatch.

## Folder Roles
- `Sessions/`: chronological record of play, usually the best source for what actually happened
- `Quests/`: active objectives, open questions, and operational leads
- `People/`: NPCs, PCs, aliases, and identity revelations
- `Organisations/`: factions, houses, cults, and political groups
- `Locations/`: cities, planes, ruins, regions, and campaign sites
- `Items/`: artifacts, materials, books, drinks, and magic objects
- `Events/`: visions, festivals, historical beats, and special scenes
- `Deities/` and `Symbols/`: religious, mythic, and symbolic context

## Local Writing Patterns
Observed patterns in `Enter the Dungeon`:
- Session notes are often rough, tactical, and operational. They may include plans, rolls, NPC intel, and follow-up tasks.
- Season summaries are often more polished and explanatory than individual session notes.
- Many entity notes start as bullets and may later gain sections like summary, themes, role, relevance, or known structure.
- Dutch and English coexist naturally, sometimes in the same note.
- Frontmatter is useful but loose. Existing notes use both `alias` and `aliases`, inline tags and list tags.
- Some notes are intentionally tiny because their main job is linkability and canon anchoring.

When editing, preserve these patterns rather than smoothing them into one house style.

## Editing Guidance For This Campaign
- Read the relevant season summary and the latest related session note before editing canon-heavy notes.
- Preserve the campaign's mixed Dutch/English style. Match the language already used in the local note.
- Keep existing note names and folder names exactly as used, including spellings like `Organisations`.
- Preserve wiki links aggressively. This campaign depends on dense cross-referencing.
- Do not resolve open mysteries as fact unless the user explicitly wants speculation or new canon drafted.
- For in-progress arcs, prefer sections like current status, open threads, important leads, and unresolved questions.
- Keep quest notes practical. A short, useful quest note is better than a polished but vague one.
- Keep session notes operational and chronological. Do not rewrite them into prose unless asked.
- When improving a note, add structure around the existing facts rather than discarding the raw material that produced them.

## Character And Canon Safety
- Identity twists matter here. Preserve aliases, hidden identities, and revealed names carefully.
- Do not collapse uncertain relationships or theories into definitive statements.
- When updating a person or faction note from a session, add only what the session clearly supports.
- If an event is a vision, rumor, belief, or propaganda, present it that way.
- If a character note contains both immediate facts and later campaign-level interpretation, keep that distinction legible.

## Preferred Note Patterns
For new or expanded notes:
- Use concise frontmatter with tags when the note is a person, location, organization, or item.
- Preserve the note's existing frontmatter style when editing; do not normalize `alias` vs `aliases` unless there is a specific reason.
- Prefer bullets for factual notes and summaries.
- Use short summary sections for season or arc overviews.
- Link every important proper noun that already exists as a note.
- If a note is still sparse, a strong stub is acceptable. Do not pad it with invented detail.

Common useful section patterns in this folder:
- Locations: setting, known structure, relevance, important people
- Character notes: appearance, campaign summary, current themes, party role, or key facts
- Arc notes: overview, major beats, open quests, unresolved threads

These are patterns, not hard requirements.

## Link And Continuity Maintenance
High-value maintenance in this campaign often means:
- linking new NPCs, locations, and items from session notes
- updating quest notes when a lead becomes active or resolved
- expanding a location or person note once a session establishes durable facts
- making sure season summaries point to the most important notes in the arc

Avoid busywork linking. Add links where they improve recall and navigation.

## High-Value Agent Work In Enter the Dungeon
- Build or update season summaries from session notes.
- Map faction relationships around `[[Neonetheril]]`, `[[Cormyr]]`, `[[De Commissie]]`, and related groups.
- Track active quest state and unresolved leads.
- Expand sparse lore notes using confirmed details already present elsewhere in the campaign.
- Identify canon gaps, naming inconsistencies, or notes that should link to each other.

## Avoid
- Retconning events without instruction.
- Rewording away tactical detail from session notes.
- Converting theories into facts.
- Mass language normalization.
- Replacing dense notes with shorter versions that lose operational value.
- Turning every stub into a polished article when the source material does not support it.

## Useful Starting Points
When you need campaign context fast, start with:
- `Sessions/Sessions.md`
- the current season summary
- the most recent session note
- the relevant quest note for the active arc
