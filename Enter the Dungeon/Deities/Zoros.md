# Zoros

[[Zoros]] appears in the campaign as a storm-linked divine or quasi-divine power centered on chaos, destructive force, and the pursuit of power.

## Core understanding
Early northern lore presents Zoros as destructive elemental power in its least restrained form.

From [[Panath Dawnfist]] and the [[Thunukalathi clan]]:
- Zoros is tied to raw elemental might.
- The barrier to this power is thin in places like [[Icewind Dale]], especially around storms, mountain passes, and glaciers.
- Zoros tempts people who seek power for its own sake.
- [[Moman the Destroyer]] is remembered as someone who fell to Zoros and pursued domination, leaving only devastation behind.

## Relationship to Talos
Zoros is repeatedly treated as related to [[Talos]], but not identical in a simple way.

Current best understanding:
- Zoros is at least an aspect or interpretation of Talos.
- In practice, Zoros represents destruction without restraint, or destruction pursued for power and control.
- Multiple notes suggest that this may overlap with, or be a mask for, [[Zvoraxian the Primordial|Zvoraxian]].
- This fits later campaign evidence that a false storm power is competing with the truer face of [[Talos]].

## Doctrine and rhetoric
The surviving Zoros material points to a very recognizable ideology:
- embrace chaos
- endure the storm
- follow power
- safety and freedom through power
- self-reliance and ambition

This appears in several places:
- the prayer found in the chardalyn mine
- propaganda and self-help style writing in [[Waterdeep]]
- the cult activity in [[Suzail]]
- military and diplomatic circles tied to [[Neonetheril]]

## Symbols and marks
Zoros is strongly associated with purple markings and bodily signs.

Known examples:
- purple storm-bands in [[Torden]]'s visions
- the phrase of the [[Uitverkorene]]: those marked by Zoros into the flesh are the embodied envoys of their god
- followers on the airship recognizing a marked individual as potentially chosen
- Cormyrese cult members and even the king being linked to purple bands

## Zoros in Cormyr
By Season 7, Zoros is no longer just mythic background.

In [[Suzail]]:
- the [[Oud Heiligdom]] has effectively become a Zoros temple
- the cult is described as unusually new, but already influential
- its teachings include self-reliance and a charged, unsettling ritual atmosphere
- lectures on Zoros are also held in the [[Ambassade van Neonetheril]]
- the cult appears connected to the [[Koninklijke crypte]], the embassy, and the wider political realignment in [[Cormyr]]

> Glorie aan oud Netheril  
> Glorie aan het uitverkoren volk  
> Zoros toont ons het pad  
>
> Omarm de chaos  
> Doorsta de storm  
> Volg de macht  
>
> In de macht zijn we veilig  
> In de macht zijn we vrij  
> Volg Zoros' pad  
>
> Glorie aan het uitverkoren volk

This is one of the clearest statements of how Zoros is framed by his followers: chosenness, chaos, storm-endurance, and liberation through power.
