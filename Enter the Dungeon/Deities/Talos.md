# Talos

[[Talos]] is understood in the campaign as a major storm and elemental deity, tied to destruction, upheaval, and renewal.

## What is known
- According to [[Humbert]], Talos is a god of the outer planes and the elements.
- He is described as one of the stronger gods.
- He is associated with unrest, disaster, and renewal.
- A common idea attached to him is: pray to Talos and he leaves you alone.
- Humbert also describes him as a god of balance, which is more nuanced than a purely evil storm god.

## Talos in the Feywild pantheon
During the visit to the [[Pantheon]] in Season 5:
- Talos is depicted as a wild, strongly human-faced storm figure.
- The statue shows him with lightning in one hand and robes that dissolve into cloud forms.
- He is less popular than some other powers, but worship of him is open and accepted.
- [[Eldric Stormcaller]] describes Talos as:
  - destruction
  - clearing away
  - the chance for new life after ruin

## Aspects and cultural readings
A recurring theme is that different cultures understand Talos through different aspects.

Confirmed examples in the campaign:
- [[Aros]]: destruction directed toward protection, discipline, and right intent
- [[Zoros]]: raw or corruptive destruction, power, chaos, and domination

This makes Talos less a single flat doctrine and more a divine force interpreted differently by different peoples.

## Campaign tension
There is strong evidence that not every power claiming Talos is truly Talos.

- In the Feywild, [[Eldric Stormcaller]] distinguishes the blue aspect as [[Talos]].
- He identifies the purple aspect as [[Zvoraxian the Primordial]], a primordial pretending to be an elemental god.
- This strongly suggests that at least part of what is presented in the world as storm-divinity is actually a false or corrupted imitation.

## Connection to Torden
Talos is one of the core divine forces shaping [[Torden]]'s arc.

- Torden repeatedly channels explicitly Talos-linked power, such as `Wrath of Talos`.
- His lightning scars, divine markings, and storm visions all deepen over time.
- The campaign increasingly treats his connection to Talos as real, but contested by rival or adjacent influences such as [[Zoros]] and [[Zvoraxian the Primordial|Zvoraxian]].
