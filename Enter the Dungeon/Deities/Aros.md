# Aros

[[Aros]] is presented as the disciplined, protective, and rightly-intended expression of storm or destructive power.

## Core understanding
From the teachings of [[Panath Dawnfist]] and the [[Thunukalathi clan]]:
- Where [[Zoros]] is, [[Aros]] is also present.
- Destruction can be used for protection.
- The victory over Zoros is not in denying power, but in channeling it correctly.
- Aros is about intent.
- Zoros leaves force and scars behind, but Aros tempers that force into proper form.

Panath compares this to hot steel that must be tempered into a good weapon.

## Relationship to Talos
Aros appears to be an aspect, cultural reading, or truer face of [[Talos]], rather than a completely separate deity.

Current best reading:
- [[Talos]] is the broader storm-and-destruction divinity.
- [[Aros]] is the interpretation of that power when it is directed by discipline, responsibility, and protective purpose.
- This likely places Aros much closer to the real Talos than [[Zoros]] is.

## Role in Torden's arc
Aros is not as institutionally visible as Zoros, but it appears in some of the most personal divine imagery around [[Torden]].

Known signs:
- In Torden's dream after the glacier expedition, his markings include two blue bands associated with Aros and one purple band associated with [[Zoros]].
- This suggests that the tension in his storm-marked arc is not simply between good and evil, but between different uses of the same destructive force.

## Importance
Aros matters because it prevents the campaign's storm theology from collapsing into "destruction bad".

Instead, Aros represents the idea that:
- power is real
- destructive force cannot simply be denied
- what matters is the intention, restraint, and purpose behind it
