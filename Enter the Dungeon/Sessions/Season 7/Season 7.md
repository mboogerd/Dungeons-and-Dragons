# Season 7 Summary

> [!note]
> This is an **in-progress season summary**. Season 7 is not finished yet, so this note summarizes the arc so far and treats current threads as active.

Season 7 opens in immediate crisis. The party returns from [[Ythryn]] only to learn that [[Alma]] has been captured in [[Cormyr]] and that [[Rince]] has gone in after her. What follows is a fast-moving covert operations season: hijacking a [[Neonetheril]] airship, infiltrating [[Suzail]], surviving a train heist, building a local resistance network, and mounting a high-risk extraction from the embassy itself.

## Overview

Compared to Season 6's ruin-delving and ancient magic, Season 7 is much more focused on live political conflict. The war with [[Neonetheril]] is no longer mostly indirect. CSI is now operating inside a hostile foreign capital where state power, cult activity, espionage, diplomacy, and magitech all overlap.

The season so far centers on [[Suzail]], the embassy of [[Neonetheril]], the strange role of [[Zoros]] worship in the city, and the possibility that the political transition in [[Cormyr]] is part of a deeper hostile realignment. The first sessions are not about solving the whole Cormyr conspiracy yet; they are about getting people out alive, building intelligence quickly, and learning how deep the rot goes.

## Major Beats

### Immediate crisis after Ythryn
- Season 7 begins directly off the Season 6 cliffhanger.
- [[Rince]] sends a panicked message that [[Alma]] has been captured and that he is going in after her.
- [[Matthew]] redirects CSI immediately toward [[Cormyr]] and provides them with Alma's report and the lead that an airship can get them there much faster than overland travel.
- The tone is urgent from the start: this is a rescue mission before it is anything else.

### Hijacking the airship
- CSI tracks down a [[Neonetheril]] airship near the [[Ardeep forest]].
- Rather than attacking directly, the party uses impersonation, social deception, and staged authority to seize control of the ship.
- They successfully bluff the crew, learn more about the ship's magitech systems, identify [[Chardalyn]]-powered energy cells, and then eliminate the crew when the time is right.
- This is an important turning point because the party is no longer just reacting to enemy airships; they are now using one.

### The voyage to Cormyr
- The airship journey reinforces several campaign threads at once:
  - Neonetheril's military-magical infrastructure is becoming more legible.
  - The party confirms Alma is imprisoned in [[Suzail]].
  - [[Zoros]] influence is visible even inside enemy ranks.
  - CSI is itself now known enough that the enemy keeps files on their associates and links them to [[Waterdeep]] and the [[Sluitsteen]].
- The trip also sharpens the sense that this is a war of logistics, information, and mobility, not just battlefield confrontation.

### Arrival in [[Suzail]]
- The city is presented as wealthy, controlled, and polished on the surface, but increasingly distorted underneath.
- CSI sees signs of industrialization, magical infrastructure, heavier state presence, and a tension between old royal order and new systems of power.
- [[Matthew]]'s local safehouse contact, [[Maelis]], becomes central as the party establishes itself in the city.
- The embassy, the royal crypt, the Zoros cult presence, and the unknown successor to the Cormyrean throne all remain in the background as connected pressure points.

### The train heist
- Learning that prisoners are scheduled for transfer by train, CSI shifts from infiltration to interception.
- The party scouts the line, studies the wagons, identifies the prison layout, and plans around Modron ticket systems, onboard staff, patrols, and wagon access.
- The actual heist becomes a blend of mystery, improvisation, and violence.
- CSI successfully frees [[Rince]] from the prison wagon, survives an onboard fight involving cult-aligned guards, an assassin, a black knight, and summoned elementals, and escapes via [[Word of Recall]].
- This is the first major tactical success of the season, but it does not solve the larger Suzail situation.

### What Rince reveals
- Back at the safehouse, [[Rince]] explains what happened during the failed embassy infiltration with [[Alma]].
- The pair had tried to use a religious gathering and diplomatic access as cover.
- Instead, they were identified, separated, and subjected to imprisonment and torture.
- Rince's account strongly suggests they were expected or betrayed, reinforcing the idea that the embassy and the local cult or political structure are tightly coordinated.

### Building the embassy rescue
- Session 3 pivots into a structured preparation phase rather than a direct attack.
- CSI and [[Maelis]]' network gather intelligence, cultivate rumors, forge papers, learn rituals, observe patrols, and map the embassy's internal structure.
- The campaign briefly becomes a proper spy operation:
  - identifying personnel
  - measuring heat and exposure
  - exploiting unrest around [[Zoros]]
  - using disguises and misinformation
- This section does a lot to define Season 7's identity: less dungeon crawl, more pressure-cooker covert mission.

### Embassy infiltration and Alma rescue
- CSI finally infiltrates the embassy under cover, using invisibility, forged identities, and manipulated guards.
- They find [[Alma]] imprisoned below the embassy and successfully extract her.
- The escape exposes more of the real enemy structure in the building, including the ambassador [[Arkady Mirov Karsin]] and the dangerous veteran or enforcer [[Serafim]].
- [[Rince]] proves highly effective during the breakout, and the party escapes with [[Word of Recall]] before the embassy can fully collapse on them.
- This is the season's second major tactical success: both Alma and Rince are now back with CSI.

## Season Themes
- Rapid-response espionage under wartime pressure
- The consolidation of [[Neonetheril]] influence through diplomacy, religion, and logistics
- [[Cormyr]] as a soft target being politically reshaped rather than simply invaded
- The spread of [[Zoros]] ideology through institutions, transport, and state-adjacent structures
- Rescue operations that gradually reveal a much larger conspiracy

## Major Character Threads

### Alma and Rince
- They are the catalyst for the season.
- The season so far is built around first losing them, then recovering them.
- Their capture reveals how dangerous and well-prepared the embassy's internal apparatus really is.

### Torden
- Torden's visible connection to storm power remains a major liability in enemy spaces tied to [[Zoros]].
- The party takes active steps to hide his markings.
- His role in the season so far is interestingly double-edged: both divine asset and obvious risk.

### Zeno
- Zeno does a huge amount of the social and operational steering this season.
- He repeatedly solves problems through deception, improvisation, and command presence rather than raw force.
- Season 7 continues to show him as one of CSI's most effective operators in hostile political environments.

### Gadget / Kashet
- Kashet's technical role expands again through ship systems, train logistics, infiltration support, and magical problem solving.
- He is consistently one of the party members translating enemy magitech into usable tactical advantage.

### Lyra
- Lyra is heavily involved in visual deception, social cover, reconnaissance, and precise magical violence.
- Her combination of elegance and brutality fits the tone of the season especially well: covert until the moment force becomes necessary.

## Important Introductions and Revelations
- CSI can now operate an enemy airship, at least provisionally.
- [[Neonetheril]] maintains operational dossiers on CSI and their known associates.
- [[Suzail]] contains overlapping systems of concern:
  - the embassy
  - the royal crypt
  - the [[Zoros]] cult or chapel
  - the new royal order
- Prisoner transport and embassy security are much more structured than simple guard duty; they are part of a disciplined state apparatus.
- [[Arkady Mirov Karsin]] and [[Serafim]] emerge as major antagonistic figures in the Cormyr arc.
- At the end of the current notes, a small magitech device is found hidden inside Torden's armor, suggesting surveillance, tracking, or internal compromise.

## Notable Sessions
- [[S07E01]]: panic response to Rince's message, airship seizure, and flight toward Cormyr
- [[S07E02]]: arrival in [[Suzail]], safehouse contact with [[Maelis]], train reconnaissance, and successful rescue of [[Rince]]
- [[S07E03]]: Rince's debrief, intelligence-gathering phase, embassy infiltration, and successful extraction of [[Alma]]

## Open Quests
- Investigate the wider [[Cormyr]] conspiracy beyond the rescue operations.
- Determine what is really happening around the [[Koninklijke crypte]].
- Trace the connection between the [[Koninklijke crypte]], the [[Oud Heiligdom]], and the [[Ambassade van Neonetheril]].
- Establish whether king [[Alidane Thorne-Obarskyr]] is compromised, complicit, or being used.
- Identify how deep [[Neonetheril|Neonetherese]] influence runs in [[Suzail]]'s political and logistical systems.
- Debrief [[Alma]] fully and act on any intelligence she gathered before and during captivity.
- Understand the purpose of the magitech device hidden in Torden's armor.
- Decide whether and how CSI can safely keep using the captured enemy airship.

## Promising Leads
- [[Koninklijke crypte]]
  - Still the clearest unresolved physical mystery in the city.
  - Directly tied to Alma's earlier investigation and to suspicious nightly activity.
- [[Oud Heiligdom]]
  - Links the Zoros sect to both local citizens and embassy-facing activity.
  - Already proven useful as an access point into the embassy network.
- [[Arkady Mirov Karsin]] and [[Serafim]]
  - The rescue confirmed both as central hostile actors rather than background names.
  - Serafim in particular escaped and remains an immediate threat.
- [[Lord Haldrim Crownsilver]]
  - Alma flagged him early as politically suspicious.
  - He remains one of the better leads for elite-level collusion inside Cormyr.
- [[Professor Emeric Koldov]]
  - Connects magitech research, genealogy, and Obarskyr bloodlines.
  - Strong candidate for a softer target than the embassy itself.
- [[Maelis]]' network
  - [[Dorn Velak]], [[Retean Coln]], [[Silra Moonfan]], and [[Davven]] provide ways to expand local intelligence instead of acting blind.
- The hidden magitech device
  - Could reveal tracking, surveillance, internal compromise, or a larger Neonetherese operating method.
- The captured airship and `Protocol Luminarch`
  - Together they offer mobility, impersonation opportunities, and insight into how Neonetheril structures command and transport.

## Active Unresolved Threads
- What exactly is happening in [[Cormyr]] beyond the embassy rescue
- The role of the new king, [[Alidane Thorne-Obarskyr]], and whether the succession crisis is manipulated from within
- The significance of activity around the royal crypt under [[Suzail]]
- The full structure of the embassy's command chain and how far its influence reaches into the city
- The role of the [[Zoros]] cult in the broader political project
- What information Alma recovered, what she learned in captivity, and what can still be acted on
- The purpose of the magitech device found hidden in Torden's armor
- Whether CSI can keep using the captured airship without drawing overwhelming retaliation
- How much of the city now knows or suspects that the embassy has been infiltrated twice in quick succession

## End State

At the current stopping point, CSI has:
- seized an enemy airship
- reached [[Suzail]] quickly instead of losing weeks to travel
- recovered [[Rince]] from prisoner transfer
- recovered [[Alma]] from the embassy
- established a local resistance or safehouse foothold through [[Maelis]] and her network

But the larger Cormyr operation is clearly not over.

Season 7 currently feels like the opening phase of a deep urban espionage arc. The immediate rescues have succeeded, but they have only exposed how much larger the conspiracy is: diplomacy, cult infrastructure, state security, transportation networks, and royal succession all appear to be part of the same hostile pattern.
