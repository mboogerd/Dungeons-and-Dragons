# Season 6 Summary

Season 6 begins as the [[Silverymoon]] crisis turns into open war, then pivots into a personal rescue mission against [[Feldressyx]], and from there opens into a deep Netherese expedition in and beneath the [[Reghed gletsjer]]. What starts as a city-defense and revenge arc becomes a descent into ancient ruins, failed magical civilization, body horror, ritual locks, and the buried city of [[Ythryn]].

## Overview

The first part of the season resolves the immediate Silverymoon emergency. Drow infiltration, stolen artifacts, and the weakening of the [[Mythal]] erupt into a direct attack on the city. CSI helps repel the invasion, rescues key defenders, and learns that multiple hostile actors are operating in parallel: Drow forces, covert saboteurs, and [[Feldressyx]] with his own opportunistic agenda.

That agenda becomes brutally personal when [[Alais Palana]] is kidnapped. The party pursues [[Feldressyx]] to [[Mt Hotenow]], infiltrates and dismantles his lair, rescues prisoners, defeats his lieutenants, and ultimately kills the dragon. That victory gives the party breathing room, treasure, and allies, but also immediately redirects the campaign toward an even larger mystery: locating lost interplanar knowledge in a buried Netherese ruin.

The rest of the season is the Ythryn expedition. CSI travels to the north with [[Rince]], uncovers the ruins under the glacier, navigates the Caves of Hunger, and eventually reaches [[Ythryn]], a fallen flying city. There they piece together how the city fell, what [[Iriolarthas]] has become, and how a ritual or security protocol governs access to the city's deeper magical core.

## Major Beats

### Silverymoon under attack
- The gray-market and infiltration threads from Season 5 pay off as Silverymoon is openly attacked.
- Drow incursions, sabotage nodes, portal infrastructure, and stolen magical assets all converge in one coordinated strike.
- CSI helps defend the city, stabilize the [[Mythal]] situation, and fight through the High Palace during the invasion.
- [[Alustriël Silverhand]] and [[Taern Hornblade]] are saved at a critical moment, though only barely.
- The theft of the [[Crystal of Ath'Nir]] and the role of [[Scarx the Scalebinder]], [[Felix Lansen]], and their allies deepen the sense that multiple factions are exploiting the chaos.

### The kidnapping of [[Alais Palana]]
- In the middle of Silverymoon's crisis, [[Feldressyx]] escalates by abducting Lyra's mother.
- The season becomes much more personal from that point onward.
- CSI travels to [[Neverwinter]] and then to [[Mt Hotenow]] to confront the dragon at the [[Frozen Forge]].

### Assault on the Frozen Forge
- The party survives traps, kobold warrens, false courtesies, environmental hazards, and Feldressyx's performative cruelty.
- They rescue [[Alais Palana]] and other captives from the [[Crystalline reliquary]].
- The lair is full of signs of vanity, collecting, magical appropriation, and domination rather than simple hoarding.
- CSI kills [[Feldressyx]], defeats [[Scarx the Scalebinder]], negotiates with surviving factions in the lair, and seizes a large hoard and several important items.
- This is one of the season's decisive victories and closes the dragon-rescue arc cleanly.

### Shift to ancient Netherese exploration
- Reports from [[Rince]] and the need for a "translation key" for interplanar travel redirect the party toward the buried ruins under the glacier.
- CSI returns north, sabotages a Neonetheril airship, and enters the Caves of Hunger.
- The campaign tone shifts heavily from war and rescue into arcane archaeology, horror, and ruin exploration.

### Caves of Hunger and the road to Ythryn
- The party encounters remnants of Netherese infrastructure, half-functioning magical spaces, predatory horrors, Drow scavengers, and Neonetheril competitors.
- They meet [[Hemal Void]], a tragic remnant of an older age with ties to [[Ariana]] and the closure of interplanar portals.
- Hemal's final moment gives the party both emotional weight and magical charms, while confirming that the old portal network and the current anchoring/interplanar research are deeply connected.
- Zeno begins physically transforming under the influence of [[Vlagomir]]'s spear, growing larger and stronger over time.

### Ythryn itself
- Once in the fallen city, the season becomes a slow reconstruction of what happened to Netheril here.
- The party learns that [[Ythryn]] was held aloft by a [[mythallar]], that [[Iriolarthas]] and the Wizards of the Ebon Star experimented with an ancient spindle, and that this caused the city's catastrophic fall.
- The city is now inhabited by broken constructs, Nothics, magical remnants, trapped entities, undead remnants of arcanists, and surviving systems that still partly function.
- CSI explores towers tied to different schools of magic, collecting hints for a ritual/security sequence that seems necessary to access the deeper heart of the city.

### Ritual hints, corruption, and the city as puzzle
- The central Ythryn arc is not just combat exploration; it is a ritualized puzzle spread across the ruins.
- The party gathers instructions such as:
  - shielding the heart with a wand from the Nether Oak
  - summoning flame in the palm
  - giving or drawing out secrets
  - using ash, poison, masks, and circles in specific symbolic ways
- This makes Ythryn feel like a city built by archmages who encoded access through magical symbolism, not just locks and keys.
- Along the way, the party deals with enchantment effects, trust fractures, hallucinations, disease-like magical influence, and localized horrors.

### Iriolarthas and the breakthrough
- CSI eventually reaches deeper into the central complex and confronts [[Iriolarthas]], now a demi-lich-like horror.
- The battle is brutal and nearly fatal.
- The party wins, recovers major magical knowledge and items, and learns crucial facts:
  - the [[Ythryn mythallar]] can be attuned by up to eight mages
  - the obelisk can potentially rewind time to avert catastrophe
  - a **staff of power** can serve as the required power source
  - the force field around the spire and the mythallar can be deactivated with the command word `Olostin`
- This is the season's biggest Ythryn breakthrough and closes the expedition arc with major answers, even if it also opens dangerous new possibilities.

## Major Character Threads

### Lyra
- The season becomes highly personal through the kidnapping of [[Alais Palana]].
- Lyra's ties to the [[Feywild]], [[Ariana]], and the emotional pull of elsewhere continue to deepen.
- Her personal stakes broaden from family and home to questions of where she actually belongs.

### Torden
- Torden continues to drift deeper into the storm-power tension around [[Talos]], [[Aros]], and related destructive forces.
- His magic becomes more visibly marked by lightning and divine force.
- He also gets several explicitly emotional moments around grief, care, confession, and responsibility.

### Zeno
- Zeno's arc in this season is unusually dramatic.
- He first leaves briefly because of Sierra, then returns for the Ythryn expedition.
- Through contact with [[Vlagomir]]'s spear, he begins transforming into something giant-like, growing physically larger and stronger over time.
- His storyline in Ythryn is both magical and bodily: not just knowledge gained, but identity changing.

### Gadget / Kashet
- Kashet's dragon identity, ambition, technical brilliance, and jealousy around knowledge all get stronger focus in this season.
- He repeatedly drives the expedition through magical analysis, infiltration, improvisation, and artifact handling.
- Season 6 strongly reinforces that he is no longer just support; he is one of the principal arcane engines of the party.

## Important Introductions and Revelations
- [[Feldressyx]] is defeated.
- [[Scarx the Scalebinder]] is defeated.
- [[Hemal Void]] is encountered and lost.
- [[Ythryn]] is confirmed as a crashed Netherese city with surviving magical systems.
- [[Iriolarthas]] is identified and defeated in demi-lich form.
- The spindle/obelisk/time-reversal thread becomes explicit.
- The city's ritual-access structure, tied to the schools of magic and symbolic acts, becomes a central mechanic.
- The expedition confirms that ancient Netherese portal/translation knowledge may still be recoverable.

## End State

By the end of Season 6, CSI has:
- saved [[Silverymoon]] from immediate collapse
- rescued [[Alais Palana]] and killed [[Feldressyx]]
- broken through into the deeper secrets of [[Ythryn]]
- defeated [[Iriolarthas]] and recovered major magical knowledge
- learned that the city's mythallar and obelisk may be capable of world-shaping effects

Season 6 ends not with a quiet resolution, but with a strong handoff into the next arc:
- the party escapes Ythryn's immediate danger,
- returns via teleportation circle,
- and receives a panicked message from [[Rince]] that something has gone badly wrong in [[Cormyr]] and that [[Alma]] has been captured.

So Season 6 functions as both a completed arc and a hinge point. It resolves the Silverymoon and Feldressyx crises, delivers the major Ythryn revelations, and then launches the campaign into the next phase of the war.
